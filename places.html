Bầu Trúc Pottery Village


Bầu Trúc ancient pottery village, in Phuoc Dan town, 10 km south of Phan Rang - Thap Cham city center. This is considered as a living museum about the traditional pottery craft of the Cham people in Ninh Thuan province.

Nam Cương Sand hill


Located about 8 km from Phan Rang - Thap Cham city to the southeast, Nam Cuong sand hill with an area of 700 hectares is an interesting destination that tourists cannot ignore when coming to Ninh Thuan. Many people compare Nam Cuong sand dunes to the beauty of a shy girl nestled behind villages, bamboo ramparts and vast grape fields. Located in the living area of the Cham community, sand dunes are an integral part of the Cham's life here. Day by day, the Cham people walk on the sand dunes to go to the sea, to the fields, to school, to the market or to the mosque…. It can be said that all activities in their daily life are associated with this sand hill.

From Wikipedia

Ba Mọi Vineyard


About 7km from Phan Rang - Thap Cham, but Uncle Ba Moi's grape farm in Phuoc Thuan commune is always bustling with tourists from near and far to visit and explore. All visitors to the vineyard can eat grapes, drink syrup, wine for free and talk with Uncle Ba Moi. Uncle Ba often shares with visitors how to identify Ninh Thuan grapes with other imported grapes, how to make wine products and the benefits brought by Ninh Thuan grape wine.

Special food


Local culinary specialities that attract people are bánh canh chả cá (bánh canh with fish paddies), bún cá sứa (rice vermicelli with jelly fish) and bánh xèo mực (squid pancakes).
